About

Architectural designer based on Florida's 30A corridor.

I'm an architectural designer at Chancey Design in Seagrove Beach, working primarily on 30A — Alys Beach, Grayton, Seaside, and Point Washington. My projects span luxury residential, commercial mixed-use, urban planning and design guidelines, and the occasional restaurant that wasn't supposed to be mine but ended up being mine. I see projects through from first sketch to construction documents, with the occasional quasi-judicial hearing presentation along the way.

I studied architecture at Auburn, where my thesis was a 3D-printed artist residency in Kyoto. That project is also where I learned that constraints tend to make better buildings than freedom does, and that's most of what I've been exploring ever since. In my work, in the side projects on this website, and in the tools I build.
